Project 01 · Python · SQL · Power BI

AI Predictive Attrition & Workforce Risk Model

Classification model surfacing per-employee attrition risk
PythonSQLPower BI
+
Business Problem

HR only found out about attrition risk after an employee resigned — retention conversations were reactive, not targeted.

Data & Tools

Python (pandas, scikit-learn), SQL, Power BI. Inputs: tenure, engagement survey scores, performance ratings, manager-change history, absence patterns.

Workflow
  1. Extracted and joined workforce records from HRIS via SQL into a single employee-level table.
  2. Cleaned and engineered features in Python — tenure bands, rolling engagement trend, manager-change flags.
  3. Trained a classification model to output a per-employee attrition risk score.
  4. Validated model output against known historical leavers before rollout.
  5. Published risk scores into a Power BI dashboard, refreshed on a schedule, segmented by department and manager.
Outcome: Gave HR business partners a ranked, evidence-based list to prioritise retention conversations, instead of reacting after resignations were already submitted.
Project 02 · SQL · Excel · Power BI

HR Attrition Analysis & Cost-of-Turnover Model

£312K in annual hidden turnover costs surfaced
SQLExcelPower BI
+
Business Problem

Leadership knew attrition "felt high" but had no costed figure to justify investing in retention.

Data & Tools

SQL, Excel, Power BI. Inputs: salary data, recruitment spend, onboarding cost estimates, departure records.

Workflow
  1. Pulled departure and salary data via SQL; built a cost-per-departure formula in Excel combining recruitment, onboarding, and productivity-ramp cost components.
  2. Applied the formula across all departures in the reporting period to produce a total cost figure.
  3. Segmented results by tenure band, department, and performance band in Power BI to show where cost concentrated.
  4. Cross-checked the top-line number against payroll figures for sanity before presenting.
Outcome: Surfaced £312K in annual hidden turnover costs not visible in standard headcount reporting, with a department-level breakdown leadership could act on.
Project 03 · Excel · Power BI

Recruitment Pipeline & Hiring Efficiency Dashboard

Time-to-hire cut 36 → 14 days
ExcelPower BI
+
Business Problem

Recruiters and hiring managers couldn't see where candidates were dropping out of the funnel or why time-to-hire was creeping up.

Data & Tools

Excel, Power BI. Inputs: ATS export — stage-by-stage candidate movement, timestamps, source channel.

Workflow
  1. Exported raw stage-by-stage pipeline data from the ATS.
  2. Cleaned and restructured it in Excel into a funnel format (applied → screened → interviewed → offered → hired), calculating conversion and dwell time per stage.
  3. Built a Power BI dashboard breaking the funnel down by role, source channel, and recruiter.
  4. Identified that drop-off concentrated in external-channel hires, and modelled the impact of shifting more roles to internal recruitment.
Outcome: Time-to-hire fell from 36 to 14 days after the resulting process changes; the internal-recruitment shift showed potential to cut external hiring costs by up to 96%.

Project 06 · SQL · Excel · Power BI

Workforce Capacity Planning & Headcount Forecasting

18% improvement in resource allocation accuracy
SQLExcelPower BI
+
Business Problem

Headcount planning was done departmentally and ad hoc — leadership had no way to stress-test resourcing decisions before committing budget.

Data & Tools

SQL, Excel, Power BI. Inputs: current headcount, attrition rate, hiring lead time, department growth targets.

Workflow
  1. Extracted current headcount and historical attrition data via SQL.
  2. Built scenario models in Excel covering growth, steady-state, and reduction cases, driven by adjustable assumptions.
  3. Surfaced the models in Power BI so leadership could toggle scenarios and see resourcing gaps by department.
  4. Flagged two departments where the model showed structural understaffing risk not visible in standard headcount reports.
Outcome: Improved resource allocation accuracy by 18% and gave leadership a stress-tested basis for hiring decisions.
